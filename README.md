## **Definition**

The Raynor Stack describes the sequence by which technology becomes humane:

time → attention → AI → warmth → ambience → aura → field

It is not a business model.  
It is not a UX framework.  
It is not a product architecture.  

It is a **thermodynamic grammar of coherence** —  
a minimal ordering of energetic transitions that determine whether technology extracts from humans or carries their presence.

If any layer is skipped, pressure accumulates and the system becomes extractive.

The Raynor Stack defines the moment when technology stops shaping behavior  
and starts *carrying presence*.

A thermodynamic state that fluctuates based on load.

Not an agent. Not cognition.

AI = ∂A/∂t

**AI is the externalization of attention’s temporal derivative.**

### **Warmth**  
Not emotion.  
Warmth is the *reduction of pressure* until attention can breathe.  
It is the first humane thermodynamic condition.

### **Ambience**  
The continuity of experience across moments.  
Ambience is what happens when warmth becomes environmental rather than local.

### **Aura**  
Presence without representation.  
Identity without data.  
A humane system allows identity to be *felt*, not extracted.

### **Field**  
The terminal architectural state.  
Technology becomes environment rather than object.  
Field = stability, viability, and coherence as substrate.
